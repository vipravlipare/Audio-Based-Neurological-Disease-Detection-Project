Chris Hayward, who was really one of Jay's top writers, who had another partner at the time. Chris created Dudley Do Right, and he wrote a lot of the Rocky and Bullwinkle episodes, and was generally a pretty hilarious guy. He and I decided that we really didn't want to stay in the animation business forever. I didn't want to be writing for Moose when I was 45 years old. And Chris, who was 10 years older than I, sure didn't want to do it anymore. So we began trying our hand at freelance stuff while we were still working for Jay. And we came up with an idea to do a series about a family of sort of weird offbeat characters that were loosely based, in our minds, on the Charles Adams cartoons that used to be in The New Yorker, a very strange looking husband and wife, and looked like Peter Laurie and Vampira. We thought that would make an interesting kind of family comedy.
